Board Quest - Case Study

Native App for iOS & Android

Description: An app that allows users to find games that their friends and family would enjoy.
Focus: low, mid, and high-fidelity wireframes; mobile gestures; user feedback; mockups

My Process

Objective

To design the iOS and Android version of a mobile app of your choice that solves a specific user problem.

Research Requirements

Analysis of 2 to 3 competitor apps.

Design Requirements

  • Must follow iOS and Material Design guidelines
  • Native mobile design patterns used
  • Clearly defined typographic hierarchy
  • Appropriate use of color
  • Appropriate use of haptic and sound feedback for interactions
  • User gestures annotated
  • UI must be intuitive
  • Consistent UI design applied across all screens

Problem

  • Users are looking to plan a game night (the day of or when planning ahead).
  • Users are looking for potential gifts for holidays and birthdays.
  • Users are searching for new content to add to their arsenal of games.
  • Users are searching for games that their friends and family would enjoy.

Solution

  • Create a native app that makes for easier access, where users do not have to rely solely on having internet access while users are out and about.
  • Reviews and details on the games will help users to determine what games they would like to play.

User Flow

  • Used FigJam for the user flow in this project.
  • The final screens overall were identical to what is shown in this user flow, however, added additional menu bar screens.
  • These screens showed what the menu bar looked like, once the drawer is open.

    For the iOS screens used:

  • SF Pro font
  • Referenced the iOS - Human Interface Guidelines

    For the iOS screens used:

  • SF Symbols for most of the icons in the top and bottom navigation
  • UI elements that were more rounded in shape

    For the Android screens used:

  • Roboto font
  • Referenced Material.io for guidance
  • UI elements were more square in shape

    For the Android screens used:

  • Most of the icons were pulled from the Resources page in Material.io
  • Addition of shadows such as with the Android cards
  • Looked at how the mobile gestures differentiated between both iOS and Android screens.
  • Looked at how gestures differed between call to action buttons, selecting icons, text fields, and swiping.

User Feedback

  • (iOS) "I noticed in the reviews that it's sorted oldest reviews first. Idk if it would be helpful to see a pop up that shows how you can sort them. Like by highest score or more recent. Kind of like how you had the "..." on the previous screen to show "Share" and "Add to My Games". I like the color though and how the buttons on the bottom turn blue to let you know which screen you are on." - Mike N.
  • (iOS) "It's very straightforward. I like the colors too. The fonts are nice and big." - Delaney D.
  • (Android) "This one seems all good to me, maybe just bigger font in that yellow menu would be nice." - Niam I.
  • (Android) "Overall I thought it was great. It was not immediately clear what the app does for me - maybe add 'board game' to slogan? Wasn't clear that I could click profile. Wasn't sure how to add a game to 'my games'. Wasn't sure how to add reviews. Easy to navigate. All menu and tiles felt intuitive. On main home, the 'Most Popular' tile click leads me to a categories page rather than the most popular games (what I expected)." - Kyle B.
  • (Android) "Looks really cool. The review page and summary page don't make it super obvious the first object is the score. Maybe need to add a star icon or make it more obvious that's the score." - Bilal B.

Challenges

  • Difficulties coming up with the original concept.
  • Coming up with ways to differentiate the screens for iOS and Android.
  • Learning Sketch while working on this assignment.

Workaround

  • Jotted down a list of things I enjoy and was able to ideate from this.
  • Referencing the iOS - Human Interface Guidelines and Material.io
  • Online resources such as: YouTube videos, sketch.com
  • Referencing prior chapters from the Intro to UI Design and UI Immersion portions of the CareerFoundry course.

Final Showcase

The original concept differed from the final results in that:

  • There was no like/favorited icons added to the games screens or next to the game names.
  • Although there was a screen that gave you a brief synopsis of the game, doesn't go into details on how to play the game.
  • Originally came up with concept to add suggestions based on prior likes/ favorited games, however, did not include this in the final screens.

Takeaways

  • For future iterations of the app, would play with the thought of using different color schemes for the iOS and Android screens.
  • Would also look to adding additional screens based on prior feedback received from the test users.
